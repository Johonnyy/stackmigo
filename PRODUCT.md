# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners and operators of businesses that live and die on the phone: home and field services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing) and medical/dental practices first, then any local business where a ringing phone is a customer. They are busy, practical, and skeptical of hype. They have been pitched "automation" and "AI" before and been burned by robotic phone trees. They judge a vendor by whether it obviously understands how their day actually works — the 2 AM emergency, the lunch rush, the solicitor calls — not by adjectives.

## Product Purpose

stackmigo is a B2B telecommunications service: AI receptionists and automated answering that pick up every call to a business, 24/7. It books appointments, screens callers, dispatches emergencies, routes to the right person, and texts the owner the details. It exists because missed calls are missed revenue — most people who hit voicemail hang up and call the next number — and small businesses miss the most calls exactly when it matters most: after hours, during the rush, when every line is busy. Success looks like an owner reading the page and thinking "this clearly answers the calls I'm losing, and it was set up by someone who gets my business."

(Background, not to be used as copy: the business previously positioned as security audits for AI-built apps; that framing is fully retired. The telecom/answering offering is the product now.)

## Brand Personality

Sharp, plain-spoken, and credible. Three words: precise, dependable, unhyped. Voice is the seasoned operator who knows exactly how many calls you're losing and answers them without drama. Confident enough to be specific about real calls. Never robotic, never salesy, never condescending.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI SaaS**: purple/indigo gradients, glassmorphism, floating blobs, the big-number hero-metric template, gradient text. The thing every AI startup ships.
- **Telecom clichés**: headset-wearing stock-photo agents, cartoon phones and speech bubbles, "press 1 for..." menus rendered as a feature, dated call-center imagery.
- **Cute / playful**: mascots, rounded bubbly illustrations, pastel consumer friendliness. Undercuts a tool a business depends on.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the calls, don't describe them.** The proof is real transcripts of calls the receptionist handled — the after-hours emergency booked, the appointment set, the solicitor screened. Evidence over adjectives.
2. **Respect the operator's time.** The audience is busy and practical. Be specific, concrete, and fast to the point. Plain language about real money (missed calls, booked jobs).
3. **Sound human, look exacting.** The product's promise is a receptionist that doesn't sound like a machine; the interface earns that promise by being crafted, not templated.
4. **One signal, used with discipline.** A single accent marks the live line and the one action that matters, the way an "on-air" light does. Rarity is the point.
5. **Editorial clarity carries the weight.** Strong typographic hierarchy and structured statements do the work; decoration does not.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort, ship-fast. Reasonable defaults: legible contrast, keyboard navigability, semantic structure, and respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Don't gate launch on a full WCAG audit; tighten toward AA after launch.
